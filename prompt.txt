ATLAS — 10 mission prompts to try
==================================================================
Paste any of these into the mission box at http://127.0.0.1:8000
(start it with `python launch.py`), or use them with the API/scripts.

Each mission makes the CEO hire a DIFFERENT, mission-appropriate team — that
is the whole point: general-knowledge agents that self-organise into whatever
the mission needs. The [mode] hint suggests a communication topology so the
set as a whole exercises every pattern; you can run any mission under any mode,
or pick "Compare all three" to watch the coordination differ side by side.

------------------------------------------------------------------
 1. Design and spec a privacy-first smart doorbell.                 [hierarchical]
 2. Plan a weekend coffee festival for 5,000 attendees.             [compare all three]
 3. Build a minimal habit-tracking mobile app.                      [mesh]
 4. Write a go-to-market plan for an AI note-taking app.            [hierarchical]
 5. Produce a research brief on the state of solid-state batteries. [group]
 6. Plan and outline a six-episode podcast series on space.         [group]
 7. Plan the opening of a specialty coffee shop in a college town.  [compare all three]
 8. Design a 30-day social campaign for a sustainable sneaker brand.[mesh]
 9. Design a four-week beginner curriculum for learning Python.     [group]
10. Design the core gameplay loop and economy for a puzzle game.    [mesh]
------------------------------------------------------------------

Why these:
- Missions with clear sub-domains (engineering / design / marketing / ops /
  legal ...) produce the richest org charts and the deepest recursion.
- [mesh]  suits work where disciplines must cross-talk (eng <-> design).
- [group] suits brainstorm / consensus work (a writers' room, a syllabus).
- [compare all three] runs the same mission three ways and shows the metric
  deltas — fewest messages = least coordination overhead, lowest elapsed = fastest.

Notes:
- No GROQ_API_KEY -> deterministic offline mock (reproducible; same team every run).
- With a Groq key -> roles are emergent and tailored to each mission.
- Caps: headcount <= 12, depth <= 3 (hard); token budget is metered + shown.
