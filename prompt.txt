ATLAS — 10 mission prompts to try
==================================================================
Paste any of these into the mission box at http://127.0.0.1:8000
(start it with `python launch.py`), or send them to POST /api/run.

Each mission makes the organisation hire a DIFFERENT, mission-appropriate team
— that is the whole point: general-knowledge agents that self-organise into
whatever the mission needs, led by an agent NAMED for the mission (e.g. a coffee
festival is run by a "Festival Director"). The team then collaborates in group
meetings to produce the result.

------------------------------------------------------------------
 1. Design and spec a privacy-first smart doorbell.
 2. Plan a weekend coffee festival for 5,000 attendees.
 3. Build a minimal habit-tracking mobile app.
 4. Write a go-to-market plan for an AI note-taking app.
 5. Produce a research brief on the state of solid-state batteries.
 6. Plan and outline a six-episode podcast series on space.
 7. Plan the opening of a specialty coffee shop in a college town.
 8. Design a 30-day social campaign for a sustainable sneaker brand.
 9. Design a four-week beginner curriculum for learning Python.
10. Design the core gameplay loop and economy for a puzzle game.
------------------------------------------------------------------

Why these:
- Missions with clear sub-domains (engineering / design / marketing / ops /
  legal ...) produce the richest org charts and the deepest recursion.
- Broad, open-ended briefs let the lead agent decide the team shape, so you see
  genuinely emergent roles rather than a fixed template.

Notes:
- Requires a GROQ_API_KEY (real LLM only — there is no offline mock).
- Roles are emergent and tailored to each mission; the top agent's title is
  derived from your prompt (e.g. "Book Fair Manager", "Festival Director").
- Communication topology is GROUP — the team collaborates in shared meeting threads.
- Caps: headcount <= 12, depth <= 3 (hard); token budget is metered + shown.
